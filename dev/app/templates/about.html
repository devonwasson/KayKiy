About KayKiy

  • What is this? This is an interactive website designed to challenge your perception of campus attitude both on and off YikYak.

  • What is YikYak? YikYak is an app that can be downloaded on most smart phones which operates as a sort of local twitter. People can post messages and anyone within a small radius of that person can see what they post. It is very popular on college campuses. This website is not affiliated with, nor does it endorse, YikYak or their affiliates.

  • What's the point of all of this? The idea is to get you thinking. As you guess on a post, you will either be right or wrong. As you guess, you are encouraged to reflect on the answers. Does the rest of the campus share your views and opinions? Should you reevaluate your opinions? Should the campus start to shift its attitude? These are the types of questions that should come up every time you interact with a post.

  • Who made this? This website was designed by Devon Wasson, a Computer Science and Engineering major at Bucknell University, and advised by Bucknell University's Professor Evan Peck (evan.peck@bucknell.edu). Devon will be working as an intern with Amazon Robotics this summer and currently does freelance web design for the Bucknell University office of Campus Activities & Programs.

  • Where did this data come from? Data was collected from YikYak once every five minutes for the entire month of April in 2015 at Bucknell's campus. Data was stored while taking note of the actual post, when it was posted, when it was observed, how many votes it had at the time, and a few other trivial points.

  • Tell me more about the graph that appears. The graph shows the lifespan of a post on YikYak (specifically, the post that you just guessed on). The blue line is the most recent line, and the green lines are from previous posts. The graph shows change in votes over time (in minutes) and allows you to build your own sample data set to get an idea of how posts generally work on YikYak.

  • What's next? While collecting data, both posts and comments were collected. Everything on this website only reflects the actual posts, but I would like to do something involving the comments at some point. I'm just not sure what/if I'll ever have the time.
